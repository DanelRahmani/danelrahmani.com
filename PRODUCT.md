# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters, exchange coordinators, potential Axiom Digital clients, and fellow students who land here from LinkedIn, a CV link, or search. They arrive with one question — "who is Danel Rahmani?" — and give the page under a minute to answer it. Secondary audience: readers of the Notion-powered notes and friends browsing the travel photos.

## Product Purpose

The personal site of Danel Rahmani: International Business student at the University of Groningen (Honours College, Waseda exchange 2026–27), founder of Axiom Digital, and part of the family business Rahmani's Gordijn Kledingatelier. It is résumé, writing surface (Notion CMS), project showcase, and photo journal in one. Success: a visitor leaves with a clear, memorable impression of a serious, worldly, self-starting person — and can reach him in one click.

## Brand Personality

Tailored, worldly, precise. The voice of a well-cut garment: quiet materials, exact seams, one bold thread. Warm but not casual; ambitious but not loud. The family atelier (fabric, thread, tailoring) is the brand's physical world — the site should feel like a tailor's label sewn into good cloth, not a developer template.

## Anti-references

- The unmodified Tailwind "Spotlight" template look (this site's ancestor) — zinc-gray text, generic card grids, template sameness.
- Developer-portfolio clichés: terminal aesthetics, matrix greens, "Hi, I'm X 👋" heroes.
- 2023-era AI landing grammar: tracked uppercase eyebrows on every section, icon tiles above headings, purple-blue gradients, gradient text.
- Beige-and-muted-slate timidity — the creme background must read as a chosen material (cloth), carried by committed maroon, not as default warmth.

## Design Principles

1. **Cut from one cloth** — every surface (home, notes, projects, images, about) shares the same materials: creme/aubergine grounds, maroon thread, hairline seams. Consistency of voice over consistency of treatment.
2. **The label is the luxury** — typography does the branding. A high-contrast display serif over a precise sans; meta detail set small and exact, like a woven label.
3. **Structure is information** — rules, columns, and sequence encode something true (a timeline is a timeline, a contact sheet is a contact sheet). No decorative scaffolding.
4. **Motion like good tailoring** — barely seen, perfectly finished. Fast ease-out transitions, one orchestrated moment per page at most, everything honoring reduced-motion.
5. **Show the world, not the stack** — photos, books, places, and businesses carry the personality; tech-stack talk stays in the project cards where it belongs.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.2 AA: body contrast ≥ 4.5:1 in both themes (watch muted text on creme), visible focus rings site-wide, full keyboard operability (photo strip, lightbox, mobile nav), `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for every animation, alt text on all photography.
